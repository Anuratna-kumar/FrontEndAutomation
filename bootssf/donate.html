Why to Donate ?

   In India, almost 62 million children (48 percent) across all income groups are stunted.Stunting, or chronic malnutrition, is accompanied by a host of problems - weak immune systems, risk of sickness and disease, arrested cognitive and physical development, and a greater risk of dying before age 5.


  Nearly 27% of the total deaths in India happen with no medical attention at the time of death,according to the 2013 civil registration data released by the Census directorate.


  Less than half of India's children between the age 6 and 14 go to school. A little over one-third of all children who enroll in grade one reach grade eight. At least 35 million children aged 6 - 14 years do not attend school. 53% of girls in the age group of 5 to 9 years are illiterate.
